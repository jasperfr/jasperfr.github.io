Q: What's the H number supposed to represent?
A: It's an ordinal based on the Hardy Hierarchy, explained here.
Basically, it's a layered ordinal whereas ω represents the "number past the base", eg in base 10, 11 becomes ω + 1.
At ω9 + 9, the next number turns into ω^2. This is because ω10 in base 10 does not exist, and becomes ω * ω, aka ω^2.
Moving on, this number eventually reaches ω^2 * 9 + ω9 + 9, which then transforms to ω^3
And so on, and so on... Until ω^9 * 9 + ω^8 * 9 ... ω^2 * 9 + ω9 + 9 is reached.
Then the ordinal becomes ω^ω!
"Ordinal layers" after that are ω^ω2, ω^ω2+1 ... ω^ω2+9 ... ω^ω9+9, ω^ω^2, ω^ω^2+1 ...
Until you reach ω^ω^9*9+ω9+9 + ... , which transforms into ω^ω^ω! This is the first uncountable ordinal.

Q: What's the difference between this version and the original Ordinal Markup?
A: This version, contrary to OM, tries to avoid most given timewalls by being a bit more forgiving in terms of grinding.
This version also tries to be a little more comfortable with styling and navigation since OM can be a bit of a labyrinth (especially after singularity).
